## Figure 4 — `deep_dive_flow`

- **Gist:** [gist](https://gist.github.com/beccajcarlson/eb7ce1015ff21ec0329472e33a9ea713) · `swh:1:rev:506d29ab3052576e6db9b33782e55b5d975483de`
- **Bundled TSV:** _(SVG mockup — no data TSV)_

**Figure 4. The per-gene deep-dive pipeline runs in three sequential stages, producing one validated record.** Triage-candidate genes flow top to bottom; box color encodes the engine performing each step. Stage 1, evidence retrieval (deterministic, no LLM): public databases build a candidate corpus, a per-paper Haiku 4.5 abstract triage selects papers worth fetching, tiered open-access full-text recovery is attempted, and a Sonnet 4.6 selector yields a trimmed evidence ledger. Stage 2, block builders (Sonnet 4.6): a concurrent fan-out emits surface-evidence, surface-biology, evidence-grade, and cross-focus risks blocks. Stage 3, synthesizer reads every block draft plus the Stage 1 ledgers, deterministic features, and triage prior, then emits the executive summary, structured filters, and confidence rationale. Each validated record is published at roughly $2–3 per gene.

## Supp S8 — `bench_topology_vs_universe`

- **Gist:** [gist](https://gist.github.com/beccajcarlson/f724dd328f48566a354f0294109a7337) · `swh:1:rev:53dde26c2bcf3fb7d8c5db78945480e29b1d4db1`
- **Bundled TSV:** `bench_topology_vs_universe.tsv` (4,429 rows · 184.1 KB)

**SurfaceBench over-samples the membrane topologies where databases disagree, so its measured accuracy is a conservative floor on full-catalog performance.** The per-class composition of the hand-curated benchmark is compared against the full shipped accessible set across nine membrane-topology classes, with each class tested by an exact two-tailed binomial test of the benchmark count against the population proportion, Bonferroni-corrected across the nine classes. The benchmark is significantly enriched for glycosylation-site-bearing (+20.5 pp) and GPI-anchored (+14.1 pp) proteins and depleted for multi-pass transmembrane proteins (−11.2 pp). Because the benchmark is deliberately weighted toward ambiguous, disagreement-prone topologies and away from the easy multi-pass case, accuracy measured on it is a lower bound on full-catalog accuracy.

**Casting a wider literature net surfaces several times more papers per gene, but most of the additional papers are unreachable, which is why the broader strategy was not adopted.** Two discovery strategies were compared on the same randomly sampled genes through the same retrieval chain: the production strategy, which searches EuropePMC and adds machine-annotated PubTator mentions, NCBI's curated gene-to-publication links, and standing topic queries; and a broad alternative issuing one gene-name query plus method-keyword queries against the OpenAlex index, which also reaches preprints and non-PubMed journals. OpenAlex discovered far more papers per gene, but reachability collapsed on the larger pool, adding mostly inaccessible literature at proportionally higher cost, so production discovery was kept.
