**Across model tiers and amounts of supplied context, triage accuracy saturates near 98% well before cost does, so the cheapest configuration reaching that plateau is the economical production choice.** Each point is one combination of model (Claude Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, or Opus 4.8) and supplied context (gene alone; with resolved identifiers; or identifiers plus a PubMed or web-search tool). The horizontal axis is the projected cost of triaging all 19,324 protein-coding genes once (log scale); the vertical axis is mean accuracy on the 147-gene benchmark, scored with partial credit. Cost is built from average tokens sent and returned per call at Anthropic's May 2026 list prices, with shared instructions charged once at the first-use rate and reused cheaply, and each web search billed at one cent. Accuracy plateaus near 97-98% across the Sonnet and Opus tiers; moving from Sonnet to Opus costs roughly five times as much for no gain, and adding a search tool raises cost without reliably helping. The production sweep uses Sonnet 4.6 with identifiers, matching Opus-tier accuracy at the lowest cost on the frontier.
