Executive summary

EGFR is a single-pass type I transmembrane receptor with exceptionally well-documented plasma membrane accessibility. Live-cell flow cytometry across >44 cancer cell lines, surface biotinylation with mass spectrometry, non-permeabilized immunofluorescence, and IHC with explicit membranous scoring criteria—combined with cryo-EM/X-ray structures of the ECD and multiple approved/clinical therapeutics engaging that domain—establish high-confidence surface accessibility. Expression is broad across solid tumor types and multiple normal epithelia. Key caveats are ligand-induced internalization that transiently reduces surface density, a predicted secreted isoform, and junctional/ciliary subdomain enrichment in polarized epithelia.

Therapeutic engagement

stage: approved_drug
description
Multiple approved and clinical-stage therapeutics engage EGFR at the cell surface. Cetuximab (FDA-approved for colorectal and head/neck cancers) binds the EGFR ECD directly, confirmed by crystal structure (PDB: 1YY9) and used as an EY-photocatalyst vehicle for surface labeling on live A549 cells. Matuzumab (Phase II) ECD complex is resolved (PDB: 3C09). A bispecific antibody targeting EGFR and NKG2D was tested on BxPC-3 pancreatic cancer cells by live-cell flow cytometry. Anti-EGFR nanobody EgB4 engages the full ectodomain (PDB: 7OM4). Small-molecule TKI gefitinib is also documented in surfaceome profiling context.
surface-form rationale
EGFR is a single-pass type I transmembrane receptor; all documented therapeutics (cetuximab, matuzumab, bispecifics, nanobodies) engage its large extracellular domain (ECD, aa25–645) displayed at the plasma membrane. A predicted secreted isoform (HPA annotation) exists, but all therapeutic programs in this ledger target the membrane-anchored form. Crystal and cryo-EM structures confirm the ECD is the drug-binding surface.

Contradicting evidence (1)

cell_line_specific_absence severity: low
Flow cytometry surface staining on live, non-permeabilized HCT 116 colorectal cancer cells shows minimal EGFR surface expression, corroborated by near-zero RNA expression in CCLE (log2[TPM+1]=0.0). This indicates that EGFR is essentially absent from the surface of this specific cell line.
likely explanation
HCT 116 is a well-documented EGFR-low/negative cell line and was deliberately used as a negative control to validate assay specificity. The absence of surface EGFR in HCT 116 reflects a cell-line-specific transcriptional silencing rather than any general property of the EGFR protein, which is robustly surface-expressed in many other cell types and tumors.
