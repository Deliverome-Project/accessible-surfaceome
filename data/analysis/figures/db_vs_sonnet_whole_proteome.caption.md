**No single surface database recovers more than a fraction of the genome-wide accessible surfaceome that the triage agent identifies.** Because no hand-curated truth exists at genome scale, agreement is measured across all 19,324 protein-coding genes against the triage agent's own surface call as the reference. That reference is the production two-tier verdict — a first pass over each gene's literature followed by a targeted second-pass rescue of ambiguous negatives — which rates 2,547 genes definitively surface ("yes"), 1,879 conditionally surface ("contextual"), and 14,898 non-surface ("no"). Soft credit applies on the positive side: a database "surface" call counts as agreement against either a definite or a conditional agent call, and a database "non-surface" call counts only against an agent "no." Among the individual databases, agreement with the agent ranges from 91% (UniProt) and 89% (SURFY) down through 80% (CSPA), 80% (GO cellular component) and 76% (the Human Protein Atlas); the permissive union calling any one of the five reaches 84%, and the strict five-way intersection 78%. The databases fall hardest on conditionally surface proteins, where individual recovery drops to 8–40% (and ensemble recovery as low as near zero), reflecting the absence of any explicit cell-state-dependent category in the source databases. Overall agreement is higher here than on the disagreement-enriched benchmark cohort, since the whole proteome is dominated by genes all callers agree are non-surface.
