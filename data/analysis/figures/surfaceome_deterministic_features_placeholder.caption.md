**Across the deep-dived surfaceome, the eleven precomputed deterministic features distribute as expected for bona-fide surface proteins, and the pattern already tracks the real deep-dive confidence tier.** Each deep-dived gene is faceted by its real deep-dive surface-accessibility tier, using the top two tiers as-is and pooling only the two weakest: canonical (the high-confidence surface tier), likely, low (low/moderate accessibility on weak evidence), and uncertain / no (the ambiguous-to-negative tiers combined). Genes that are Sonnet-positive but not yet deep-dived carry a pending tier and are excluded from this per-tier comparison, since they have no deep-dive verdict to compare. The 4×3 grid compares one deterministic feature per panel: DeepTMHMM transmembrane-helix count and protein length (violins), and the fraction carrying a signal peptide, an extracellular N-terminus, an extracellular C-terminus, a 1:1 mouse ortholog, a 1:1 cyno ortholog (Ensembl Compara), a predicted homo-oligomer state (Schweke 2024), an alternative isoform with a topology differing from canonical, at least one predicted surface-accessible binding site, and a concerning paralog whose extracellular domain is ≥40% identical (a potential antibody cross-reactivity risk) (fraction bars — a violin of a 0/1 value is meaningless). The surface-binding-site fraction rises steeply with confidence (canonical 64% → uncertain/no 4%), as expected for genuine surface targets. Topology and every deterministic feature shown here are now sourced from the deep-dive RECORDS (full coverage per deep-dived gene), fixing the prior DeepTMHMM-M1-only coverage bias where the low, uncertain, and no tiers were 70–94% missing. Each value is a tool-derived entry on the immutable deterministic_features record block, reproducible from the bundled per-gene TSV. PRELIMINARY: a partial sweep of the roughly 5,128 candidate genes has been deep-dived so far, so the per-tier rates should be read as provisional until the sweep completes.
