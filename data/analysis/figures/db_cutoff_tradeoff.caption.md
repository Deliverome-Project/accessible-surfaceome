**Re-deriving each source database's surface-inclusion rule against SurfaceBench changes which proteins two of the five sources admit, while three are already well-calibrated.** Each panel shows one source database; points are alternative inclusion rules of increasing permissiveness, plotted by the number of proteins each would admit (horizontal axis, log scale; further left is stricter) against classification accuracy on SurfaceBench, the 147-protein benchmark of manually labeled surface, contextually surface, and non-surface proteins (vertical axis). Each point is annotated with the fraction of true surface proteins recovered and the fraction of non-surface proteins correctly excluded, making the recall-versus-specificity trade-off explicit. The current rule and the rule recommended after the benchmark audit are marked. UniProt was loosened by additionally accepting transmembrane- and signal-peptide-annotated entries, recovering GPI-anchored and single-pass receptors it had been missing with little loss of specificity. CSPA was tightened by restricting it to high-confidence detections only, improving specificity at comparable recall. The GO cellular-component, Human Protein Atlas, and SURFY rules were already at their accuracy optimum and left unchanged.
