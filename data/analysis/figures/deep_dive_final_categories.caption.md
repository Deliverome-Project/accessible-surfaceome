**The deep-dive agent sorts the roughly 5,000 triage-positive candidates into five biology-driven surface-call buckets, with cell-state-induced presentation broken out by what triggers it.** This figure is a MOCK rendered against placeholder counts (cohort n = 5,000) and will be re-rendered once the deep-dive sweep finishes; the bucket definitions and the trigger taxonomy shown here are final. Each gene falls into one bucket. The "canonical surface" bucket collects reasons that mark a protein as a constitutive surface resident — classical receptors, membrane-spanning proteins with exposed loops, lipid-anchored proteins, extracellular-facing proteins, and stable surface-complex partners. The "likely surface" bucket collects softer, context-dependent reasons such as attachment to the surface or localization that is split between compartments. The "cell-state induced" bucket holds proteins reaching the surface only under a triggering condition, and is stacked by that trigger — oncogenic, immune, stress or hypoxia, cell death, infection, or other — ordered most to least common. The "cell-type restricted" bucket holds proteins confined to a particular tissue, and the "no" bucket holds reasons placing the protein away from the accessible surface, capturing cases the deep dive downgrades from a triage-positive call. Bar totals appear above each column; per-trigger counts appear in the legend. [Figure data and script.](https://gist.github.com/beccajcarlson/c2441f8d0314c5524463bc85a3e86612)
