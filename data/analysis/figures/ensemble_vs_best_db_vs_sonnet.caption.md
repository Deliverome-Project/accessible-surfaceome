**Supplementary Figure 5. Ensembling the five gold-standard surface databases does not close the accuracy gap to the Sonnet 4.6 triage agent.** Overall accuracy on the 147-gene SurfaceBench (soft-credit rule) is shown for six callers: the production triage agent (Sonnet 4.6 with gene identifiers; bar height is the mean of three replicates, overlaid as points with a SEM error bar), the single best-performing database (UniProt, calling a gene surface on a strict surface term, an extracellular topology assignment, a transmembrane segment, or a signal peptide), and four ensemble callers requiring at least two, three, four, or all five databases to agree. Each of the five databases (UniProt, GO cellular component, HPA, SURFY, CSPA) is applied under the surface threshold that maximizes its own accuracy here, so every caller competes at its best. Inner labels give the correct/total count. The agent reaches 97.5% (143 of 147), exceeding the best single database by 7.7 percentage points (UniProt at 89.8%, 132 of 147), with a SEM of 0.6 points across replicates. Requiring at least two databases to agree lowers accuracy to 83.7% (123 of 147), and demanding agreement from more degrades it monotonically (three or more: 68.7%, 101 of 147; four or more: 48.3%, 71 of 147; all five: 35.4%, 52 of 147). Combining the databases therefore suppresses surface calls faster than it corrects them and never recovers the proteins they jointly miss.
