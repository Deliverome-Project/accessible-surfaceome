**Supplementary Figure 5. Ensembling the five gold-standard surface databases does not close the accuracy gap to the Sonnet 4.6 triage agent.** Overall accuracy on the 147-gene SurfaceBench (soft-credit rule) is shown for six callers: the production triage agent (Sonnet 4.6 with gene identifiers; mean of three replicates with SEM), the single best-performing database, and four ensemble callers requiring at least two, three, four, or all five databases to agree. Each database (UniProt, GO cellular component, HPA, SURFY, CSPA) is applied under the surface threshold that maximizes its own accuracy, so every caller competes at its best. Requiring more databases to agree degrades accuracy monotonically: combining the databases suppresses surface calls faster than it corrects them and never recovers the proteins they jointly miss.
