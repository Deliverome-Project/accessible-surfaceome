**Supplementary Figure 1. Supplying gene identifiers, but not live retrieval tools, drives the within-tier accuracy gains in language-model triage on the 147-gene benchmark.** Bar height is the mean across replicates of overall verdict accuracy, scoring a call correct when a contextually-surface protein is labelled surface (soft-credit rule). Three Claude tiers (Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.8) are each compared under four prompt-context conditions: gene symbol alone (naive); symbol plus a cached gene summary and stable HGNC/UniProt identifiers (+IDs); identifier context plus an on-demand web-search tool; and identifier context plus on-demand PubMed retrieval. Sonnet 4.6 and Haiku 4.5 ran at three replicates per gene per condition (overlaid points; error bars SEM); Opus 4.8 ran on the naive and +IDs conditions only. Adding identifiers gives the largest gain; layering web search or PubMed on top yields no consistent further improvement on any tier. Sonnet 4.6 with identifiers is the production triage configuration.
