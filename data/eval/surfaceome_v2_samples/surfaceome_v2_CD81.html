Executive summary

CD81 is a canonical tetraspanin (4-TM, two extracellular loops) with high, direct-multi-method evidence for plasma-membrane surface accessibility across diverse human cell types. Live-cell flow cytometry on AML patient blasts (n=290) and multiple cell lines, surface biotinylation MS with >2-fold enrichment, immunogold EM on intact EVs, and biochemical topology mapping collectively confirm surface exposure of both ECL1 and the structurally defined LEL/ECL2. Expression is broadly moderate-to-high across hematolymphoid, neuroectodermal, and mesenchymal lineages, with disease-state upregulation in non-CBF AML LSCs and high-grade glioma. Consequential accessibility risks are modest: the LEL is small (~88 residues), an intracellular pool co-exists with the surface pool, and expression can be near-absent in some hepatoma lines at baseline.
