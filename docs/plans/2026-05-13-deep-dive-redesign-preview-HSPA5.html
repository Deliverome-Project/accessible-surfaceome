The DB split is real and biological: UniProt / HPA / DeepTMHMM capture the dominant ER-lumenal pool of GRP78 (the canonical chaperone state). SURFY / CSPA capture the tumor-cell and stress-induced surface fraction (which they sample preferentially because their input datasets are biased toward cancer cell lines). The deep-dive evidence below confirms both are true — the question is in which cells / contexts the surface fraction matters.

Executive summary

GRP78 is the ER chaperone BiP — predominantly lumenal in normal cells (held by KDEL retention) but with a robust, multi-citation surface fraction in tumor cells (pancreatic, multiple myeloma, prostate, glioma) and under ER stress / apoptosis. DeepTMHMM finds signal peptide only — no transmembrane anchor — so the surface story doesn't fit a classical receptor framework. Anti-GRP78 antibodies of varying specificity are in clinical-stage trials (PAT-SM6 in myeloma). Accessibility is genuinely high-state-dependent and the key risks are paralog cross-reactivity (HSP70 family is highly conserved) and uncertainty about the molecular mechanism of ER → surface translocation.

1 Surface accessibility evidence

The tumor-cell-surface case

evidence grade: direct_multi_method tumor-context-specific

Live-cell flow cytometry with multiple anti-GRP78 clones, surface biotinylation MS, and non-permeabilized IF all confirm endogenous surface GRP78 on tumor cell lines and patient-derived tumor samples. Matched normal cells show no surface signal — consistent with the DB disagreement (UniProt / HPA capture the dominant ER pool; SURFY / CSPA capture the tumor-cell surface fraction).

Therapeutic engagement (not a comprehensive landscape)

highest_stage = in_clinical_trials

Description: PAT-SM6 (Patrys) — anti-GRP78 IgM monoclonal in Phase 1 for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma [evi_T11]. Additional preclinical programs targeting surface GRP78 in pancreatic and prostate cancer [evi_T12, evi_T13].

Surface-form rationale: PAT-SM6 recognizes a conformational ECD epitope present only on cell-surface GRP78, not on the canonical ER-lumenal form (the antibody was selected against patient-derived tumor cells, not the soluble chaperone). This specificity is the entire point — GRP78 is ubiquitous as an ER chaperone, so a non-specific binder would have catastrophic on-target / off-tumor toxicity. Soluble sGRP78 in patient serum (also documented) is NOT the therapeutic target.

Not a comprehensive therapeutic landscape — just the topline signal that surface GRP78 specifically has been clinically engaged.

Contradicting evidence (typed + severity)

  • Canonical ER-lumenal localization alternative_localization severity=HIGH (for normal cells) [evi_60]

    The dominant non-tumor state. Does NOT undermine the tumor-cell surface claim — instead it reframes the context. Normal cells: surface=ABSENT. Tumor / stressed cells: surface=HIGH. Both are biologically real; the DB split reflects this.

  • N-terminal fragment "GRP78-N" isoform_conflict severity=MODERATE [evi_61]

    Some surface-detection reports show a ~50 kDa fragment rather than full-length ~78 kDa. Origin unclear (proteolytic? alternative start?). Antibody epitope matters: C-terminal antibodies may miss the surface form.

4 Paralogs HSP70 family — antibody specificity is non-trivial

The HSP70 family cross-binding problem

The HSP70 family is the textbook case where antibody specificity is hard. Members share >60% identity at the protein level and conserve the substrate-binding domain fold. Some commercial antibodies marketed as "anti-GRP78" cross-react with HSPA1A or HSPA8 — see §1 antibody validation chips for which clones were KO-validated vs. overexpression-only.

Rationale: 63–64% ECD identity to HSPA1A / HSPA8 — well above the "fold conserved, loops divergent" zone where binders typically separate. Published cross-reactivity is documented for multiple commercial polyclonals; KO-validated monoclonals (PAT-SM6) demonstrate that paralog-specific binding is achievable but non-trivial. Mitigation: require KO-validation for any anti-GRP78 reagent used in flow / IF; prefer C-terminal-targeting antibodies (HSP70 family is more divergent there than at the N-terminal ATPase domain).

6 Accessibility risks severity ≠ evidence strength

What could hide or obscure the protein

  • Partner required for surface expression? surface_expression_dependency=UNKNOWN

    Mechanism of ER → plasma membrane translocation is unresolved. Proposed mechanisms include MTJ-1 (DnaJC1) co-trafficking, KDEL-receptor saturation under ER stress, and direct insertion via GPI-anchor-like modification. None confirmed. This uncertainty is the load-bearing knowledge gap below. [evi_73, evi_74]

  • Secreted form severity=MODERATE evidence=STRONG [evi_75]

    Soluble GRP78 is detectable in serum of cancer patients and in conditioned media of tumor cell lines. Two pools may exist: surface-attached and freely secreted. Binders intended for surface engagement should be screened for activity against soluble GRP78 (sink risk).

  • Similar paralogs (HSP70 family) severity=HIGH evidence=STRONG

    HSPA1A / HSPA1B / HSPA8 are 63–64% identical and share the ATPase + substrate- binding fold. Antibody cross-reactivity is the dominant accessibility risk for this target — see §4 paralog table + §1 antibody validation chips. Mitigation: KO-validated mAbs only.

  • ECD accessibility size class LARGE

    636 aa post-signal-peptide region. The whole mature protein is potentially surface-accessible when on the surface (no membrane anchor restricts which face is exposed). Caveat: the chaperone substrate-binding domain may be occupied by client peptides, occluding some epitopes.

  • Epitope masking mechanism=[partner, conformational] severity=MODERATE evidence=STRONG [evi_77]

    Chaperone partner binding (BAG family, GRP94, J-domain proteins) and substrate clients may mask epitopes on surface GRP78. Conformational antibodies (e.g. PAT-SM6 — see §1) selectively recognize a subset of the surface pool, which may be a feature rather than a limitation depending on the application.
