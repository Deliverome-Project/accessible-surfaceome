**Supplementary Figure 9. Each source captures a different and incomplete slice of the surfaceome's membrane-topology classes, and the triage agent's coverage reaches furthest into the context-dependent architectures.** For every protein in the candidate surfaceome — the 6,585 proteins called surface by at least one of the five source databases or by the triage agent — the panels show the fraction that each source places in each of nine membrane-topology classes. The nine classes are GPI-anchored, seven-transmembrane GPCR, multi-pass transmembrane (non-GPCR), single-pass transmembrane, signal-peptide-only secreted, inner-leaflet lipidated (prenylated or myristoylated with no transmembrane segment), no transmembrane segment and no signal peptide, glycosylation-site-bearing, and transmembrane-without-signal-peptide; the first eight derive from sequence and keyword annotation and the last from a DeepTMHMM topology prediction. Seven sources are compared: the triage agent, the 794 proteins the agent calls surface that no database flags, and the five databases individually (UniProt, GO cellular component, the Human Protein Atlas, SURFY, and CSPA). For UniProt and CSPA the bars use the inclusion thresholds tuned for best agreement with the curated benchmark — the same cutoffs used in the accuracy figures — so under these thresholds UniProt covers 3,552 proteins (54%) and CSPA 993 (15%); GO cellular component, the Human Protein Atlas, SURFY, and the agent use their standard inclusion rules. The agent-only subset is correspondingly defined as the proteins the agent flags that no database flags once those benchmark-tuned thresholds are applied. Each bar is the percentage of the 6,585-protein universe a source covers within that class. The agent's coverage approximately tracks UniProt's on the well-curated architectures (GPCR, multi-pass and single-pass transmembrane) and reaches further on the context-dependent classes. Inner-leaflet-lipidated proteins — cytoplasmic-facing by anchor geometry and so not accessible from outside the cell — are admitted at low but non-zero rates by GO cellular component and the Human Protein Atlas, while SURFY, CSPA, and the agent correctly leave most of them out.
