**On the SurfaceBench genes deep-dived so far, the evidence-anchored deep dive matches the Sonnet+NCBI triage's soft-credit accuracy, and its confidence tier tracks ground-truth difficulty.** For the 27 of 147 SurfaceBench benchmark genes deep-dived so far (the intersection), each protein's deep-dive surface call and the Sonnet+NCBI triage call are scored against the curated ground-truth verdict under the soft-credit rule — a contextually-surface protein counts correct when called surface. (a) Overall soft-credit accuracy. (b) Accuracy split by ground-truth bucket (yes / contextual / no). The deep-dive bar is stacked by the tier it assigned to the correctly-classified genes — canonical / likely / low (green shades) and uncertain / no (grey), the same five-tier palette as Figure 5a — so its confidence composition is visible: ground-truth-yes genes are called canonical + likely, contextual genes likely + low, and the correct not-surface calls land in uncertain / no. Sonnet+NCBI accuracy is the mean of three mainbench replicates (one dot per replicate; the error bar is the SEM across replicates); the deep dive runs once per gene, so its bar carries no replicate spread. The deep-dive verdict counts as surface when its tier is canonical, likely, or low; Sonnet and the ground truth count as surface when yes or contextual. Deep dive and Sonnet are near-identical overall (96% vs 96%), and Sonnet's SEM overlaps the deep-dive bar in every bucket, so no difference is significant at this n. PRELIMINARY: n = 27 is small — the 'no' bucket is only 2 genes — so read these as an early signal; the estimates firm up as the sweep covers more benchmark genes.
