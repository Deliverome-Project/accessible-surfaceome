**Two-panel summary of how the deep-dive agent sorts the surface cohort.** (a) The cohort splits into three tiers under the catalog's preset predicates: `canonical` (the strict gold-standard surface tier), `likely` (the broader passes-likely surface set), and `no` (fails the likely predicate — away from the accessible surface, or too thin on evidence). (b) The composition of the `likely` tier — why those calls are only likely rather than canonical — as a sorted breakdown over the cell-type and cell-state reasons: cell-type-restricted (tissue-confined constitutive surface), cell-state induced (surface only under an oncogenic / immune / stress-hypoxia / cell-death / infection / other trigger), and the likely residual; splitting them onto their own axis keeps the small triggers legible. Bucket boundaries are the frontend catalog-preset predicates (`passesCanonical` / `passesLikely` / `passesInduced` / `passesCellTypeRestricted`), so the figure matches the interactive catalog filters exactly. **Preliminary:** computed over the ~1,197 genes deep-dived so far (of ~5,128); the `no` tier is inflated by weak-evidence records and the cell-state `oncogenic` share by tumour-associated over-flagging — partly the pretrim-cap recall bug — so treat as a preview pending the full sweep and QA fixes.
