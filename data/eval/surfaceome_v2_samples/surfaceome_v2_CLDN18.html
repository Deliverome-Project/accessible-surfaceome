Executive summary

CLDN18 (claudin-18) is a four-pass tight-junction protein with two isoforms: CLDN18.1 (lung) and CLDN18.2 (stomach/tumors). Surface accessibility is established by live-cell flow cytometry, membranous IHC across multiple tumor cohorts, 89Zr-zolbetuximab PET imaging in xenografts, surfaceome mass spectrometry, and CAR-T on-target clinical toxicity. Zolbetuximab (anti-CLDN18.2) is FDA-approved for gastric/GEJ adenocarcinoma. Key caveats: junctional subdomain restriction in normal epithelium (partially relieved in tumors), isoform selectivity requirements, and uncertain surface retention in CLDN18-ARHGAP26 fusion contexts.

grade rationale
CLDN18 surface accessibility is supported by multiple independent direct methods from multiple sources. (1) Live-cell flow cytometry (non-permeabilized): Sahin et al. 2008 (PMID:19047087) demonstrated isoform-specific antibody binding to cancer cell surfaces; PMC11712040 confirmed surface expression of both isoforms on N87 cells via live FACS; PMC12323529 and PMC12808354 provide additional live-cell FACS methodological anchors. (2) IHC with explicit membranous scoring (cytoplasmic excluded): multiple primary tumor cohorts including BTC (PMC12018027), PDAC (PMC11979426), and others document membranous CLDN18 staining. (3) In vivo functional antibody engagement: 89Zr-zolbetuximab PET imaging in xenografts (PMC12766881) showed specific tumor uptake far exceeding IgG control, confirming circulating-antibody-accessible surface epitope. (4) CAR-T on-target toxicity in gastric mucosa (PMC12579600) provides clinical human in vivo functional evidence of surface exposure. (5) Surfaceome mass spectrometry (PMC6528836) identified CLDN18 with extracellular antigenic epitopes. Collectively, live-cell flow cytometry, membranous IHC, in vivo antibody PET imaging, and functional CAR-T engagement constitute well-replicated multi-method direct surface evidence from independent sources.

description

CLDN18.2-targeted CAR-T cell therapies have shown promising efficacy in early-phase gastric cancer trials. surface-form rationale
CLDN18 is a four-pass transmembrane tight-junction protein with no known secreted or shed isoform. Therapeutics engage the membrane-anchored form exclusively, accessing extracellular loop epitopes on intact tumor cell surfaces. This is confirmed by live-cell flow cytometry (isoforms CLDN18.1 and CLDN18.2), in vivo PET imaging showing specific tumor uptake of radiolabeled zolbetuximab, and on-target off-tumor CAR-T toxicity in normal gastric mucosa.
