**Figure 1. Five gold-standard surface-protein databases agree on only 188 proteins genome-wide.** Five-way ellipse Venn over the optimized binary surface flag from each of the five sources used in this study — UniProt (n = 3,092), SURFY (n = 2,733), HPA (n = 2,050), GO CC (n = 1,998), and CSPA (n = 1,231) — built from the whole-proteome catalog (`data/processed/catalog/whole_proteome_catalog.tsv`, 19,324 protein-coding genes from the candidate universe). Each set is the union of all UniProt accessions a source labels as plasma-membrane surface under the source-specific rule documented in Methods (UniProt: strict surface terms or extracellular topology; GO CC: non-IEA annotation to a surface-relevant CC term; HPA: Enhanced/Supported/Approved reliability for Plasma membrane or Cell Junctions; SURFY: published binary classifier output; CSPA: high-confidence flag only). The central all-five intersection contains 188 proteins (< 10% of the average source's catalog), while every source contributes its own large source-private slice — most strikingly HPA (1,063 not in any other source) and SURFY (917 only shared with UniProt), reflecting their distinct evidence modalities (antibody-validated subcellular imaging and ML prediction, respectively). Region labels with fewer than 100 proteins are suppressed for readability; per-source totals in the legend are exact. The poor overlap motivates the LLM-based triage approach: no single source captures the consensus surfaceome, and unioning all five inflates membership at the cost of definitional coherence.

## Figure 4 — `deep_dive_flow`

- **Gist:** _(pending publication)_
- **Bundled TSV:** _(SVG mockup — no data TSV)_

**Figure 4. Per-gene deep-dive pipeline: three sequential stages producing one validated SurfaceomeRecord.** Schematic of the in-process deep-dive pipeline run on every triage-candidate gene (`scripts/surfaceome_v2_annotate.py` → `src/accessible_surfaceome/agents/surfaceome_v2/orchestrator.py`); arrows show data flow and box color encodes which engine performs the step. Stage 1, Evidence retrieval (deterministic, no LLM): three template-driven queries against PubMed, EuropePMC, and PubTator build a per-gene candidate corpus (median 234.5 papers/gene), then a per-paper subloop runs a Haiku 4.5 abstract triage to decide which papers are worth fetching, attempts full-text recovery through PMC JATS → Unpaywall OA PDF → DataCite landing-page scrape (each tier falling through on miss), and hands a trimmed evidence ledger to a Sonnet 4.6 selector. Stage 2, Block builders (Sonnet 4.6): nine block builders run in two parallel waves — Surface Evidence (evidence quality / detection methods / contradictions) and Surface Biology (expression, subcellular location, anatomical accessibility, accessibility modulation) fan out concurrently, while the shared Risks block reads the full fan-out to surface cross-block contradictions. Stage 3, Synthesizer (Sonnet 4.6): reads all block drafts, the Stage 1 evidence ledgers, the immutable deterministic-features payload (DeepTMHMM topology, AlphaFold pLDDT, Compara orthologs, Schweke 2024 homo-oligomers), and the triage prior, then emits the executive summary, structured filters, and confidence rationale. Every record is validated against the SurfaceomeRecord Pydantic schema and published in lockstep to disk, the viewer fallback, and public Cloudflare D1; mean wall-clock 90–180 s and $0.40–$0.60 per gene at the Sonnet 4.6 tier.

## Supp S5 — `ensemble_vs_best_db_vs_sonnet`

- **Gist:** [gist](https://gist.github.com/beccajcarlson/0104308c239fe49d91d82a1007632b27) · `swh:1:rev:05697acdef1c22b0152a82b02265dcf5fe22d355`
- **Bundled TSV:** `ensemble_vs_best_db_vs_sonnet.tsv` (4,410 rows · 855.2 KB)

**Supplementary Figure 5. Ensembling the five gold-standard surface databases does not recover the accuracy gap between any single database and the Sonnet 4.6 triage agent.** Overall accuracy on the 147-gene SurfaceBench (soft-credit rule) for six callers: the production triage agent (Sonnet 4.6 + IDs, mean across three replicates; overlaid points and SEM error bar), the best-performing single database (UniProt under its bench-optimized cutoff that accepts a strict surface term, an extracellular topology feature, a transmembrane domain, or a signal peptide), and four k+ ensemble callers that score a gene as surface when at least k of the five databases (UniProt, GO CC, HPA, SURFY, CSPA — each under its own bench-optimized cutoff per Methods) vote surface. Permissive ensembling (2+ DB) drops accuracy to 83.7% because adding majority-vote requirements suppresses single-database surface calls without recovering them through redundancy; tightening the ensemble vote monotonically degrades accuracy further (3+ DB: 68.7%; 4+ DB: 48.3%; 5+ DB: 35.4%), reaching the all-databases-must-agree floor that motivated SurfaceBench's construction in the first place. The asymmetry confirms that database disagreement on this benchmark is informative rather than noise, and ensembling cannot recover the cases where the underlying databases systematically miss or miscall the same protein.
