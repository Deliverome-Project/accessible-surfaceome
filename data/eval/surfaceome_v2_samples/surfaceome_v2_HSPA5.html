Executive summary

HSPA5/GRP78 is a stress-induced ER chaperone that translocates to the plasma membrane in cancer cells under ER stress, yielding robust, multi-method surface-accessibility evidence across leukemia, glioblastoma, and solid tumor contexts. Cell-surface biotinylation MS (AML/leukemia lines), non-permeabilized IF, flow cytometry, and functional studies with surface-selective inhibitors CBT100/200/300 collectively confirm extracellular epitope accessibility. Surface expression is state-dependent—largely absent on resting normal cells—and a secreted/EV-associated pool creates a decoy risk for systemic therapeutics.

Confidence reasoning

moderate A1 grades evidence as direct_multi_method (biotinylation MS, non-permeabilized IF, flow cytometry, functional assays), which nominally supports high confidence. However, two factors drag overall confidence to moderate: (1) strong state-dependence—A2 documents that surface HSPA5 is an ER-stress/oncogenic-transformation-induced phenotype largely absent from resting normal cells, creating high inter-tumor variability; (2) IHC on clinical cohorts (a1_evi_14, a2_evi_16, a2_evi_17) found membranous localization in only rare cases across breast, ovarian, and pancreatic cancers, indicating that cell-line-based direct evidence may not translate uniformly to primary tumors. The secreted/EV-associated pool (a1_evi_17, a2_evi_18, a2_evi_19) further complicates therapeutic engagement. Taken together, surface accessibility is real but highly context-dependent, warranting moderate rather than high confidence.

Section 1 — Surface evidence

evidence grade
direct_multi_method
grade rationale
HSPA5/GRP78 surface accessibility is supported by multiple independent direct methods across independent sources. (1) Cell-surface biotinylation + mass spectrometry on live, non-permeabilized AML and non-AML leukemia cell lines (PMC12849903; a1_evi_01–03). (2) Non-permeabilized immunofluorescence on intact cells with explicit statement that cells were not permeabilized (PMC7801745; a1_evi_15). (3) Surface biotinylation of anti-GRP78 antibodies exploiting extracellular epitope accessibility for targeted delivery (PMC9954741; a1_evi_16). Additional corroboration comes from functional assays showing surface GRP78 binds transmembrane partners (PMC13052156; a1_evi_12) and review assertions citing flow cytometry and WB surface confirmation in glioma lines (PMC12772127; a1_evi_08). A minor conflicting signal exists: IHC on a clinical cohort found only one case with detectable surface localization (a1_evi_14), and a1_evi_17 notes HSPA5 in conditioned medium (ambiguous). These do not negate the robust multi-method, multi-source direct surface evidence.

Therapeutic engagement

stage: preclinical_in_vivo
description
Multiple preclinical programs target cell-surface HSPA5/GRP78: (1) surface-selective small-molecule inhibitors CBT100, CBT200, and CBT300, developed to specifically engage only the cell-surface form on brain tumor cells (PMC13052156); (2) anti-BiP/GRP78 antibodies reported in pre-clinical trials across cancer indications (PMC9777260); (3) anti-GRP78 antibodies conjugated to streptavidin-bearing microbubbles for targeted delivery (PMC9954741); and (4) broader preclinical chemical/antibody drug candidates targeting surface GRP78 in leukemias and solid tumors (PMC12849903).
surface-form rationale
HSPA5/GRP78 exists as an ER-lumenal chaperone and a stress-induced cell-surface form. All documented therapeutic programs explicitly target the cell-surface form: CBT100/200/300 were designed to inhibit only surface GRP78 (not ER-resident GRP78); anti-GRP78 antibodies exploit surface-accessible epitopes confirmed by non-permeabilized IF and biotinylation assays. A secreted/shed form co-purified with client proteins in conditioned medium is noted but is not the therapeutic target.
