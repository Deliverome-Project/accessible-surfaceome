**Across model tiers and amounts of supplied context, accuracy on the triage benchmark saturates near 98% well before cost does, so the cheapest configuration that reaches that plateau is the economical production choice.** Each of the ten points is one combination of model (Claude Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, or Opus 4.8) and supplied context (the gene alone; with resolved identifiers; or identifiers plus a PubMed or web-search tool). The horizontal axis is the projected dollar cost of triaging all 19,324 protein-coding genes once, on a logarithmic scale; the vertical axis is each configuration's mean accuracy on the 147-gene benchmark, scored with partial credit. Cost is built up from the average tokens each call sends and returns at Anthropic's May 2026 list prices, with the shared instructions charged once at the higher first-use rate and reused cheaply thereafter, and each web search billed at one cent. The cheapest usable configuration is Haiku 4.5 with identifiers (about $35 per whole-genome pass at 90% accuracy), but accuracy plateaus near 97-98% across the Sonnet and Opus tiers; moving from Sonnet to Opus costs roughly five times as much for no accuracy gain, and adding a search tool raises cost without reliably helping. The production sweep uses Sonnet 4.6 with identifiers (about $111, 97.5%), which matches Opus-tier accuracy at the lowest cost on the frontier.
