Executive summary

GPR75 is a canonical class A GPCR with 7TM topology predicted to reside at the plasma membrane, primary cilium, and vesicles (HPA/UniProt). Expression is high in CNS neurons and moderate in pancreatic islets; peripheral tissues are low/absent. No validated direct surface-accessibility assay exists: all anti-GPR75 antibodies fail KO-specificity testing, and cryo-EM reveals a collapsed ECD eliminating the classical orthosteric pocket. Surface accessibility is biologically plausible but evidentiary support is weak-grade, driven by computational topology, database annotations, and functional CCL5/beta-cell assays rather than direct surface proteomics or live-cell flow cytometry.

Contradicting evidence (1)

antibody_conflict severity: high
All commercial and custom anti-GPR75 antibodies tested in global Gpr75 null mice exhibited non-specific staining, making any antibody-based surface-localization claims (IHC, IF, flow cytometry) for GPR75 unreliable. No validated antibody reagents exist for mouse GPR75, forcing confirmation of expression to transcript- or genomic-level methods only. This fundamentally undermines the evidentiary basis for direct surface-accessibility measurements derived from antibody-based assays.
likely explanation
GPR75 is a poorly characterized GPCR with low expression levels; cross-reactivity of antibodies with structurally related GPCRs or other proteins is a common problem in this family. The absence of validated antibodies does not disprove surface localization but means that positive antibody-based staining results cannot be trusted without orthogonal validation (e.g., epitope-tagged constructs, mass spectrometry-based surface proteomics).
