Rationale

Sonnet's synthesis across the evidence pool.

TGOLN2 (TGN46/TGN51) is a type-I single-pass TM glycoprotein residing primarily in the trans-Golgi network but constitutively cycling to the plasma membrane via PKD-dependent CARTS carriers. UniProt O43493-2 canonical topology: signal peptide (aa 1-21), N-terminal lumenal domain (aa 22-381, ~360 aa), single TM helix (aa 382-402), cytoplasmic tail with YQRL motif (aa 403-437). When at the PM, the ~360 aa lumenal domain carrying 9 N-linked glycosylation sites and mucin-like repeats is fully extracellular, making this an exposed_ecd protein at the surface. The YQRL tyrosine-based motif recruits AP2 clathrin adaptors for rapid PM internalization; retromer/retriever complexes drive retrograde transport from early endosomes back to TGN, keeping steady-state PM fraction low. Three named isoforms (TGN46, TGN48, TGN51) differ in cytoplasmic tyrosine motif complement: TGN51 uniquely retained at TGN even with mutated YQRL due to additional motifs. DB panel: 3/8 sources vote surface (SURFY ML, UniProt query, DeepTMHMM); CSPA absent (PM fraction too low for MS detection); no HPA antibody. No shedding documented; no close human paralogs (Pfam PF17818 unique to TGOLN2); no therapeutic programs reported. Cynomolgus one-to-one ortholog at 81.01% identity; concordant surface behavior likely but not independently verified.

Confidence reasoning

Multiple independent lines of evidence establish constitutive TGN↔PM cycling for human TGN46 (TGOLN2): Ponnambalam 1996 directly demonstrates cell-surface presence and endosomal return; Lujan 2024 (eLife) characterizes human TGN46 as a type-I single-pass TM protein cycling TGN↔PM in CARTS; Stephens & Banting 1999 documents constitutive cycling via AP2/YQRL; CLEM (Krentzel 2025) directly visualizes TGN46+ carriers en route to PM; Li 2025 confirms retromer-dependent endosomal return. UniProt annotates both Cell membrane and TGN membrane. The ~360 aa extracellular domain is large, N-glycosylated, and fully exposed when at PM. Conditional_surface/trafficking_cycling is explicitly cited as canonical in the task schema for TGN46. DB split (3/8) reflects predominantly TGN localization; absence from CSPA is expected given very low steady-state PM fraction. No human paralogs identified (Pfam PF17818 unique to TGOLN2 in human). No shedding documented. Confidence is high on mechanism; the low steady-state PM fraction is a practical but not theoretical limitation.
