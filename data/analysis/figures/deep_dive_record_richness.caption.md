**Each deep-dive record is information-dense, and surface-yes calls draw on a deeper evidence base than non-surface ones.** This figure summarises a gene's record along four richness axes: papers found in discovery, papers selected for full-text reading, selected papers with extracellular evidence, and total evidence records extracted. Panels (a) and (b) contrast non-surface versus surface-positive records; panels (c)–(d) show the surface-positive subset only. Dots are real values from 1,197 published deep dives, including panel (a): discovery-corpus size is a real per-gene count (median about 240 papers) rather than an earlier synthetic draw. Each violin remains a synthetic full-cohort sketch drawn around the real per-bucket mean and spread. PRELIMINARY: about 1,197 of the roughly 5,128 candidate genes have been swept so far and this is pre-QA-fix; the figure re-renders from the real per-gene counts as the sweep completes.
