**Supplementary Figure 9. Each source captures a different and incomplete slice of the surfaceome's membrane-topology classes, and the triage agent reaches furthest into the context-dependent architectures.** Across nine membrane-topology classes, the panels show the fraction of the candidate surfaceome each source covers within each class, for the triage agent, the proteins the agent calls surface that no database flags, and the five databases individually (UniProt, GO cellular component, the Human Protein Atlas, SURFY, and CSPA). UniProt and CSPA use the benchmark-tuned inclusion thresholds; the others use their standard rules. Inner-leaflet-lipidated proteins — cytoplasm-facing and not externally accessible — are over-admitted by GO cellular component and the Human Protein Atlas, but correctly excluded by SURFY, CSPA, and the agent.
