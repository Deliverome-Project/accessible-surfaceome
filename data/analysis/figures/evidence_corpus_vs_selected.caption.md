**The number of papers retained as evidence per gene grows sub-linearly with the size of the discovery corpus, and the agent's evidence-quality grade tracks the depth of the retained set.** This is a MOCK figure: every point is a synthesized gene (n = 1,500 placeholders), and all counts are stand-ins pending the full deep-dive sweep. The horizontal axis (log scale) is the number of papers found for a gene during literature discovery; the vertical axis is the number of those papers kept as evidence after the selection step. Each point is colored by the evidence-quality grade the agent assigns to the gene's evidence base, on four levels: direct evidence from multiple independent assays, direct evidence from a single assay, supportive but indirect evidence, and weak or sparse evidence. The placeholder counts were drawn to mimic the shape expected from production: corpus sizes centered near a median of about 234 papers per gene, with the fraction of papers retained shrinking as the corpus grows, so larger corpora yield proportionally less evidence. The real-data version will plot the actual discovery-corpus size, retained-evidence count, and evidence-quality grade for every gene once the genome-wide deep-dive sweep is complete.
