Contradicting evidence (1)

intracellular_pool severity: high
IHC scoring of SRC in human tumor tissue sections classifies SRC staining as cytoplasmic rather than membranous, in contrast to AXL and c-MET which are scored for membranous staining intensity. This is consistent with SRC's known biology as an N-myristoylated kinase anchored to the inner leaflet of the plasma membrane with no extracellular domain, and directly refutes plasma membrane surface accessibility in the extracellular sense relevant for therapeutic targeting.
likely explanation
SRC lacks an extracellular domain and is attached to the cytoplasmic face of the plasma membrane via N-myristoylation. IHC performed on permeabilized, fixed tissue detects this cytoplasmic/inner-leaflet pool, which is not accessible from the extracellular space. The 'membranous' scoring used for AXL and c-MET reflects their true type-I transmembrane topology with exposed extracellular domains, a topology SRC does not share.
