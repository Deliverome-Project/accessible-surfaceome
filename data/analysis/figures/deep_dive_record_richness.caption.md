**Each deep-dive record is information-dense, and the surface-yes calls draw on a deeper evidence base than the non-surface ones.** This figure summarises how much a single gene's record captures along five richness axes: the number of papers found in the discovery search, the number of papers actually selected for full-text reading, the number of selected papers carrying extracellular evidence, the number of structured catalogue filters that hold a value (of 24), and the number of precomputed structural features that are populated (of 7). Panels (a) papers found and (b) papers selected show two distributions side by side, one for records the agent judged non-surface and one for records judged surface-positive (including weak calls); panels (c) extracellular-evidence papers, (d) structured filters filled, and (e) structural features populated are shown for the surface-positive subset only. This is a MOCK figure. The dark dots are the real per-record values from 20 published deep dives (6 non-surface, 14 surface-positive), while each violin and its interquartile box are a synthetic 5,000-draw fan sketching the shape a full-cohort distribution might take. The dots in panels (b) through (e) are genuine measurements; only the violins are synthetic. Panel (a) is fully synthetic on both axes, because the size of the discovery search was never stored on these records and is drawn entirely from the reported methods-section median of 234.5 (range roughly 50 to 400).
