**On the small set of genes carried through both stages, the deep-dive's evidence-anchored reason matches the triage agent's first-pass reason about half the time, with the remaining disagreements split between same-verdict relabelling and outright verdict changes.** Each gene is assigned a short reason describing why it was or was not called a surface protein — for example a classical receptor, a context-dependent surface attachment, or a cytoplasmic protein — drawn from one shared vocabulary used at both stages. The triage agent assigns a reason from abstracts alone; the deep-dive agent re-derives one after reading full-text evidence. The matrix tabulates these paired calls, triage reason on the vertical axis against deep-dive reason on the horizontal, with cells on the diagonal counting exact agreement. Reasons are grouped into the surface-yes, context-dependent, and surface-no verdict blocks, so movement within a block (a reason swap that keeps the verdict) is distinguishable from movement across blocks (a changed verdict). Only twenty genes currently have records from both stages, so this is an early, low-count comparison rather than a stable rate: of those twenty, ten reasons match exactly, four are same-verdict relabellings, and six cross verdict blocks. The pattern should be re-examined as the deep-dive corpus grows.
