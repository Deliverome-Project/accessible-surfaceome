**Figure 3. The triage agent rescues 960 surface candidates that no source database flags.** Of the 19,324 protein-coding genes in the whole-proteome catalog, 13,698 (71%) sit in the zero-database slice where none of the five reference sources (UniProt, GO cellular component, the Human Protein Atlas, SURFY, and CSPA) marks the protein as surface-localized — here "no database flags it" means under the bench-optimized cutoffs, the same thresholds the accuracy figures use to score each database. Within that slice the two-stage Sonnet 4.6 triage agent calls 81 proteins definitively surface ("yes", panel a) and 879 conditionally surface ("contextual": state- or lineage-dependent, panel b). The agent first reviews each gene using NCBI gene and protein records; genes it initially rejects for an ambiguous reason are re-examined with a literature-search pass, which upgraded 166 of the 960 rescues (the remaining 794 came from the first pass). Bars group each verdict by the agent's stated rationale — classical receptors, multipass channels with exposed loops, GPI-anchored and stable-complex partners on the definite side; dual-localized, tissue-restricted, surface-tethered, cell-state-induced, and secretion-exposed proteins on the contextual side. Listed examples (LVRN, STEAP3, RHCE, NYX, LY96; IL15, KLK2, TIMP2, GSDME, HSPA1A, HSP90B1, HPSE) each belong to the reason category shown above them.
