Executive summary

ATP5F1B (ATP synthase β-subunit) is canonically a mitochondrial inner-membrane F1 subunit but is also detected at the plasma membrane as part of ectopic ATP synthase (eATP synthase). Multiple direct methods—surface biotinylation MS, non-permeabilized IF with orthogonal controls, live-cell flow cytometry, and super-resolution live-cell imaging—support genuine surface accessibility. Surface expression appears conditional (not ubiquitous), with highest confidence in specific cell types; CSPA/SURFY databases do not annotate it as a surface protein, reflecting its non-conventional trafficking origin.
