**Two-panel summary of how the deep-dive agent sorts the surface cohort.** (a) The deep-dived cohort placed on a five-tier confidence spectrum: `canonical` (the strict gold-standard surface tier), `likely` (the broader passes-likely surface set), then the below-likely genes split by the deep-dive's tentative call — `low` (low/moderate accessibility but weak evidence — maybe surface), `no` (leaned not-surface), and `uncertain` (ambiguous). `canonical` and `likely` are exactly the frontend catalog-preset predicates (`passesCanonical` / `passesLikely`); the low/no/uncertain split of the negatives is a figure-only refinement (the presets don't cover the negatives). (b) The composition of the `likely` tier — why those calls are only likely rather than canonical — as a sorted breakdown over the cell-type and cell-state reasons: cell-type-restricted (tissue-confined constitutive surface), cell-state-induced (surface only under an oncogenic / immune / stress-hypoxia / cell-death / infection / other trigger), and `other`; splitting them onto their own axis keeps the small triggers legible and makes clear that cell-type and cell-state are sub-buckets *of* likely. **Preliminary:** computed over the ~1,175 genes deep-dived so far (of ~5,128); the low/uncertain/no tiers are weak-evidence tentative leans — partly the pretrim-cap recall bug that deletes foundational literature — and the cell-state `oncogenic` share is inflated by tumour-associated over-flagging, so treat as a preview pending the full sweep and QA fixes.
