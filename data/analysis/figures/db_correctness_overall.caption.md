**Supplementary Figure 1. Supplying gene identifiers, but not live retrieval tools, drives the within-tier accuracy gains in language-model triage on the 147-gene benchmark.** Bar height is the mean across replicates of overall verdict accuracy, scoring a call correct when a contextually-surface protein is labelled surface (soft-credit rule). Three Claude tiers are compared — Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, and Opus 4.8 — each under four prompt-context conditions: the gene symbol alone (naive); the symbol plus a cached gene summary and stable HGNC/UniProt identifiers (+ IDs); that identifier context plus an on-demand web-search tool (+ IDs + web); and the identifier context plus on-demand PubMed retrieval (+ IDs + PubMed). Sonnet 4.6 and Haiku 4.5 were each run at three replicates per gene per condition (overlaid points; error bars are SEM across replicates); Opus 4.8 was run on the naive and + IDs conditions only. Adding identifiers gives the largest gain on Haiku 4.5 (81.6% → 90.2%), with smaller gains on Sonnet 4.6 (95.7% → 97.5%) and Opus 4.8 (95.9% → 98.2%). Layering web search or PubMed retrieval on top of the identifier context yields no consistent further improvement on any tier; on Sonnet 4.6, + IDs (97.5%) matches or exceeds both tool-augmented conditions (web 96.6%, PubMed 97.3%). Sonnet 4.6 with identifiers is the production triage configuration.
