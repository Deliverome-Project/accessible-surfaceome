Executive summary

CLDN18 (claudin-18) is a validated, surface-accessible tight-junction tetraspanin-topology transmembrane protein with two isoforms: CLDN18.1 (lung-restricted, normal) and CLDN18.2 (gastric-restricted normally, broadly ectopically activated in cancer). Multiple live-cell flow cytometry and membranous IHC datasets confirm plasma-membrane ECD exposure. Zolbetuximab approval in gastric/GEJ adenocarcinoma operationalizes this accessibility clinically. Key risks are junctional subdomain restriction in polarized epithelium, tumor-context state dependence for CLDN18.2, and CLDN18-ARHGAP26 fusion-related localization uncertainty in a minor gastric cancer subset.

Therapeutic engagement

stage: approved_drug
description
Zolbetuximab (anti-CLDN18.2 monoclonal antibody; Astellas) is approved for HER2-negative, CLDN18.2-positive gastric/GEJ adenocarcinoma, validated in Phase III trials SPOTLIGHT and GLOW. Clinical eligibility required IHC-confirmed membranous CLDN18.2 expression in ≥75% of tumor cells. CAR-T therapies and TAC (T-cell antigen coupler) constructs using anti-CLDN18.2 nanobodies (e.g., hVH6) are in emerging development. CLDN18.2 is also under investigation in PDAC, biliary tract cancers, and ovarian cancer contexts.
surface-form rationale
CLDN18 is a tight-junction transmembrane protein with no documented secreted isoform. Both CLDN18.1 and CLDN18.2 are expressed at the cell surface as membrane-anchored proteins, confirmed by live-cell flow cytometry on non-permeabilized cells. Therapeutic agents (zolbetuximab, CAR-T, TAC constructs) engage the extracellular domain of the membrane-bound form exclusively. A caveat exists for CLDN18-ARHGAP26 fusion variants, where surface localization may be altered.

Contradicting evidence (1)

isoform_conflict severity: moderate
The CLDN18-ARHGAP26 fusion protein found in a subset of gastric cancers may have altered subcellular localization relative to wild-type CLDN18.2. If the fusion protein is mislocalized away from the cell surface, therapies dependent on surface-accessible CLDN18 (e.g., zolbetuximab, CAR-T) may be ineffective in fusion-bearing tumors. This represents a caveat for assuming surface accessibility of CLDN18 in this tumor subtype.
likely explanation
The ARHGAP26 fusion partner may disrupt the transmembrane topology or trafficking signals of CLDN18, potentially rerouting the fusion protein intracellularly. However, this is a speculative concern raised in a review with no direct experimental localization data for the fusion protein; wild-type CLDN18.2 surface expression in non-fusion-bearing tumors remains well-supported.
