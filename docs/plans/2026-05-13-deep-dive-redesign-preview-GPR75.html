Executive summary

GPR75 is a Class A orphan GPCR with seven transmembrane helices. Surface localization is canonical for the topology class and DB sources agree, but endogenous protein evidence is sparse — most surface confirmation comes from HEK293 overexpression systems. Anti-GPR75 antibodies are predominantly polyclonal peptide preparations with weak validation. The three small extracellular loops (~30 aa each; ~90 aa total) make this a constrained binder-design surface. Currently of high interest as an anti-obesity target following the 2021 NEJM genetic LoF + BMI association (Akbari et al.) and Regeneron's clinical-stage program.

1Surface accessibility evidence

The orphan-receptor evidence base

evidence grade: supportive_but_indirect overexpression-dominant; few endogenous studies

GPR75 surface localization is supported by topology (7-TM GPCR) and DB consensus, but most experimental evidence comes from heterologous overexpression. Endogenous protein-level surface detection is limited — likely a sensitivity issue (low receptor density per cell) rather than a localization issue. Antibody validation is the major weakness — orphan receptors don't have well-validated commercial antibodies. CSPA's failure to detect endogenous GPR75 in surface biotinylation reinforces this read: the protein IS at the surface, but at expression levels that strain the sensitivity of standard surface-proteomics workflows.

Therapeutic engagement (not a comprehensive landscape)

highest_stage = in_clinical_trials

Description: Regeneron's anti-GPR75 program (REGN9933 + small molecules) advanced to Phase 1 for obesity following the 2021 NEJM genetic LoF + BMI association [evi_T21, evi_T22]. Multiple preclinical antibody and small-molecule programs in industry and academic labs.

Surface-form rationale: GPR75 is a 7-TM GPCR with no documented secreted form — the only physiologically relevant species is the cell-surface receptor. Therapeutic engagement (binding the receptor on intact cells) is by definition surface-form engagement. The published Regeneron candidates target extracellular-loop epitopes (mAb arm) or the TM allosteric pocket (small-molecule arm), both of which require an intact surface-displayed receptor.

Not a comprehensive therapeutic landscape — just the topline signal that surface GPR75 has reached clinical-stage therapeutic engagement.

Contradicting evidence

  • Intracellular staining patterns in some IF studies intracellular_pool severity=LOW [evi_84]

    Some studies show GPR75 staining beyond the plasma membrane (intracellular punctate, ER-like). Likely a mix of biosynthetic intermediates (normal for any GPCR) plus antibody non-specificity given the polyclonal preps in use. Does not undermine the surface localization claim — but reinforces the antibody-validation concern.

  • CSPA panel does not detect endogenous GPR75 proteomics_conflict severity=LOW [evi_55]

    CSPA's surface biotinylation MS panel doesn't capture GPR75 in any of its tissue or cell-line surfaceomes. Most likely an expression-level / sensitivity floor rather than a localization claim — consistent with overall low endogenous expression. This is what the evidence_density=LOW filter captures.

Small extracellular surface: the 3 extracellular loops (ECL1, ECL2, ECL3) plus the N-terminal extension sum to ~90 residues — much smaller than EGFR's ~620-aa ECD. This is why ecd_accessibility=small in the filters. Binder design is constrained: mAb paratopes have less area to engage, conformational recognition of the loop ensemble is harder to achieve, and small-molecule allosteric sites on the TM bundle become competitive with extracellular-loop antibodies.

4Paralogs deterministic + LLM cross-binding (low-risk family)

Class A orphans diverge in the ECD

Unlike the HSP70 family (60%+ ECD identity, MODERATE-HIGH cross-reactivity risk) or the ERBB family (45% ECD identity, LOW empirical risk), Class A orphan GPCRs diverge substantially in their extracellular loops. ECD-targeting antibodies face low cross-reactivity risk; the more pressing concern is the small ECD itself (see §3).

6Accessibility risks

A constrained extracellular surface

  • Partner required for surface expression? surface_expression_dependency=NONE

    Classical GPCRs traffic to the surface unassisted; no documented obligate partner for GPR75 trafficking. Some orphan GPCRs require RAMP-like accessory proteins — no evidence of such a requirement for GPR75.

  • Shed / secreted form NEGLIGIBLE

    7-TM GPCRs are not shed and not secreted. No evidence of soluble GPR75 fragments.

  • Small ECD (extracellular loops only) severity=HIGH evidence=STRONG

    The three extracellular loops + N-terminal extension sum to ~90 aa — much smaller than a single-pass receptor like EGFR. Binder design is constrained: paratope area is limited, conformational recognition across loops is harder, and small molecules that engage the orthosteric / allosteric TM pockets compete with antibodies on accessibility grounds. This is the most consequential structural risk for a GPR75 antibody program. Consider nanobody / VHH formats with smaller paratopes, or small-molecule modulators that engage TM-bundle pockets.

    accessibility_risks.ecd_size_assessment · { ecd_accessibility_class: Literal["large","moderate","small","minimal","none"] = "small", rationale, cited_evidence_ids } L
  • Poor antibody validation (orphan-receptor problem) severity=HIGH evidence=STRONG

    No KO-validated commercial anti-GPR75 antibody documented in the public literature. All major reagents are polyclonal peptide preps from commercial vendors with "vendor_claim_only" validation. This makes any flow / IF / IHC signal interpretation provisional until a KO-validated reagent is generated. Common path: in-house mAb development against the N-terminus or ECL2, with HEK293T-KO of GPR75 as the validation reference.

  • Low endogenous expression evidence severity=MODERATE evidence=STRONG

    Per-cell receptor density at endogenous expression levels has not been quantified. CSPA's failure to detect GPR75 in any tissue surfaceome suggests endogenous abundance is below standard MS sensitivity. For a binder program, this implies bin-counting flow assays will be near the dynamic-range floor — high-affinity reagents and amplification (e.g. dual-binders) may be needed.

  • Epitope masking mechanism=[glycan, conformational] severity=MODERATE evidence=MODERATE

    Predicted N-glycosylation at N-terminus (UniProt). Small ECD + glycan masking further reduces accessible paratope footprint. Conformational state of the receptor (active / inactive / arrestin-bound) may further occlude some loop conformations.

7What we couldn't determine

The orphan-receptor uncertainties

  • Endogenous ligand identity conflicting impact=HIGH [evi_87, evi_88]

    CCL5/RANTES and 20-HETE have both been proposed as GPR75 ligands; the field is not aligned. This caps top-level confidence at moderate. Suggested resolution: orthogonal ligand-binding studies (SPR / BLI with purified receptor), functional assays in KO-rescue systems, cross-laboratory replication of the proposed ligand-binding affinities.

  • Quantitative endogenous protein expression per cell type no_literature impact=HIGH

    Receptor density per cell at endogenous levels is unknown for hypothalamus, adipose, endothelium. Suggested resolution: in-house mAb + quantitative flow with calibration beads, or in-situ hybridization correlated with single-cell RNA-seq for relative expression mapping.

  • Mechanism by which LoF reduces BMI no_literature impact=MODERATE [evi_89]

    Akbari et al. 2021 NEJM established the human genetic link but the mechanism (CNS appetite? adipose lipolysis? endothelial energy expenditure?) remains debated. Suggested resolution: tissue-specific Gpr75 KO mouse models comparing metabolic phenotypes.

  • G-protein coupling efficacy / β-arrestin recruitment conflicting impact=MODERATE

    Reports vary on Gi / Gq / β-arrestin coupling. Suggested resolution: standardized BRET-based G-protein and β-arrestin recruitment assays with consistent ligand reference.
