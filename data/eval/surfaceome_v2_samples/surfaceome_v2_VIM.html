Executive summary

VIM (vimentin) displays confirmed cell-surface accessibility in multiple independent human cell contexts: cancer cell lines (live-cell flow cytometry, antibody 7C11-D9), Huh-7.5.1 hepatocellular carcinoma cells (surface biotinylation/WB), primary apoptotic neutrophils (live-cell flow), and senescent chondrocytes (cell-based ELISA on intact cells). Surface exposure is strongly state-dependent — preferentially upregulated in cancer, apoptosis, and senescence — consistent with VIM's canonical role as a cytoskeletal intermediate filament whose surface form (CSV) is an unconventional, non-transmembrane presentation.

Confidence reasoning

moderate A1 grades evidence as direct_multi_method across three independent assay families (live-cell flow cytometry, surface biotinylation/WB, cell-based ELISA) with zero contradicting entries. However, confidence is capped at moderate because: (1) state dependence is high — surface CSV is consistently linked to disease/stress states (cancer, apoptosis, senescence), not resting normal cells; (2) VIM lacks a canonical transmembrane domain, so surface accessibility reflects an unconventional, context-gated mechanism rather than constitutive surface expression; (3) antibody validation for several flow cytometry methods is weak (no KO controls, unspecified clones). These factors together reduce target-discovery confidence relative to a conventional surface receptor.

Section 1 — Surface evidence

evidence grade
direct_multi_method
grade rationale
VIM surface accessibility is supported by at least two distinct direct method types from independent sources: (1) live-cell flow cytometry without permeabilization using anti-vimentin mAb 7C11-D9 across multiple cancer cell lines (PMC10073919) and on primary human apoptotic neutrophils (PMID:41365250); (2) surface biotinylation (sulfo-NHS) followed by streptavidin pulldown and anti-vimentin Western blot in Huh-7.5.1 hepatocellular carcinoma cells (PMC11744697); and (3) cell-based ELISA on intact non-permeabilized senescent chondrocytes (PMID:41865378). These come from multiple independent groups and cover cancer cell lines, primary immune cells, and primary chondrocytes. No contradicting evidence (intracellular-only or secreted-only) is present in the ledger. The grade is confidently direct_multi_method.

CSV is not uniformly distributed across the plasma membrane — its surface presentation is strongly linked to specific cell states (apoptosis, senescence, oncogenic transformation) and is absent or undetectable on resting/normal-state counterparts. No specific membrane subdomain (e.g., raft, apical) is defined in the ledger, but the state-gated nature of surface exposure functionally restricts accessible epitopes.

No evidence in the ledger defines a trafficking partner required for CSV surface presentation. Vimentin lacks a canonical signal peptide or transmembrane domain; its surface externalization mechanism (caspase cleavage in apoptosis, unconventional secretion in cancer/senescence) is only partially characterised. A co-receptor dependency cannot be ruled out.

Vimentin is a type III intermediate filament protein with no canonical extracellular domain, transmembrane segment, or signal peptide. Its surface presentation is non-conventional; the accessible epitope area and orientation on the outer leaflet are undefined and likely minimal compared to classical cell-surface receptors.
