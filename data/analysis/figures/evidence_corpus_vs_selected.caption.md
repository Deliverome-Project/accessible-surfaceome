**The number of papers retained as evidence per gene grows sub-linearly with the size of the discovery corpus, and the agent's evidence-quality grade tracks the depth of the retained set.** Every point is a real published deep-dive record, not a synthetic draw. The horizontal axis (log scale) is the number of papers found during literature discovery (the EuropePMC + PubTator + gene2pubmed union, median about 240 per gene); the vertical axis is how many were selected and read full-text as evidence (median about 12). Points are colored by the agent's evidence-quality grade — direct evidence from multiple independent assays, direct evidence from a single assay, supportive but indirect evidence, conflicting evidence, or weak or sparse evidence. PRELIMINARY: about 1,197 of the roughly 5,128 candidate genes have been swept so far and this is pre-QA-fix; a handful of legacy records lack a stored discovery-corpus size and are omitted (both axes must be present to plot a point), and the weak pile is partly inflated by a pre-trim recall bug that deletes foundational literature. The figure re-renders from the real per-gene counts once the genome-wide sweep completes.
