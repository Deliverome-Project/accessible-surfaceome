Panel (a) is a verdict-flow view: one slim 100%-stacked bar per triage verdict (yes, contextual, no), split by the deep-dive's five-tier call (canonical, likely, low, uncertain, no). Triage-yes resolves to about 64% canonical plus 25% likely; triage-contextual spreads across the likely / low / no middle tiers; triage-no stays mostly no but a minority are upgraded to likely or low. Panel (b) is a confusion matrix of the triage reason (rows) against the deep-dive's re-derived reason (columns), both drawn from one shared vocabulary; reasons are grouped into surface-yes, context-dependent, and surface-no blocks so a reason swap that keeps the verdict is distinguishable from a changed verdict, and the maroon diagonal marks exact agreement (about 50% of genes). Panel (c) asks whether the deep dive agrees more with Sonnet triage or with the databases: among the genes the deep dive calls surface (tier canonical, likely, or low; n≈883), it shows the percentage also flagged surface by each source — the five catalog databases (UniProt, GO, SURFY, CSPA, HPA) plus Sonnet triage. Sonnet triage is the tallest bar at about 94%, far above the best database (UniProt at about 69%), so the evidence-anchored deep dive concords most with the upstream Sonnet call rather than with the databases. The triage verdict and reason come from the genome-wide Sonnet triage run in the public database; the deep-dive reason and tier come from the deep-dive records, and the five per-DB surface flags come from the candidate-universe catalog.
