Block-builder pipeline — how the deep-dive decomposes

The map of the v2 deep-dive. Retrieval (plan_trim_select) lands two claim ledgers; the A1 (surface-evidence) and A2 (surface expression) builders each extract one structured block from their slice. 10 builders total — 3 A1, 6 A2, 1 cross-focus — of which three are ROLLUP steps (evidence_grade, biological_context_grade, and the synthesizer) that aggregate across blocks rather than extract a single observation type. The cross-focus SHARED risks_builder consumes the MERGED A1+A2 ledger plus the deterministic ECD / homo-oligomer signals, so it runs AFTER the fan-out (not on one focus's slice) and produces the canonical accessibility_risks block the synthesizer used to author. Use this to judge whether the split into per-observation builders + rollups hangs together.

165## The judgment that matters
166 
167- **`surface_accessibility`** — pick the **best-case state** for a
168 target-discovery reader, not the worst case. If accessibility is
169 high in some state (cancer cells, activated immune cells, stressed
170 cells, polarized epithelia), set `surface_accessibility=high` and
171 use `state_dependence={moderate, high}` to flag the conditionality.
172 Don't pick `low` just because normal-cell accessibility is low —
173 that erases the targetable state. The reader filters on
174 `surface_accessibility` to find candidates, and on
175 `state_dependence` to understand whether targeting is constitutive
176 or state-gated. Canonical case for an inner-leaflet kinase: a
177 cancer-state outer-leaflet inverted form is high in cancer cells, so
178 `surface_accessibility=high` + `state_dependence=high` (not
179 `surface_accessibility=low` because normal-cell access is low).
180 Reserve `surface_accessibility=no` for proteins where the deep-dive
181 evidence does not surface a targetable state anywhere.
182 
183 **`high` requires direct evidence in hand** (topology prior alone
184 is not enough — catalog readers treat `high` as "confidently
185 surface-accessible"). **THE BRACKET** below is the single source of
186 truth — other sections cross-reference it, never restate it:
187 
188 | grade + confidence | max surface_accessibility |
189 |---|---|
190 | `direct_multi_method` | `high` |
191 | `direct_single_method` + `confidence=high` | `high` |
192 | `direct_single_method` + `confidence ∈ {moderate, low}` | `moderate` |
193 | `supportive_but_indirect` | `moderate` |
194 | `weak` / `conflicting` | `low` |
195 
196 `state_dependence` still flags conditionality when the bracket caps
197 `high` → `moderate`, so canonical-receptor signal isn't lost.
198 
199- **`state_dependence`** — captures how much the targetable surface
200 fraction VARIES by state (cell type, activation, cancer induction,
201 stress, etc.). `low` means the surface form is essentially the same
202 across the contexts the evidence covers; `moderate` / `high` mean the
203 targetable state is state-conditional and the catalog reader should
204 know that before scoping a campaign.
205 
206 **`state_dependence='low'` is forbidden when the A2 biology shows
207 state-conditional upregulation of the surface form.** Specifically,
208 pick at least `moderate` when ANY of:
209 * `filters_llm.induction_trigger != "none"` (you set this — your
210 own derivation of the dominant trigger bucket)
211 * A2's `accessibility_modulation` contains ≥3 rows with
212 `direction="increases"` (independent observations of the surface
213 form going up under some state)
214 * A2's `accessibility_modulation` contains any
215 `cell_state_trigger="oncogenic_transformation"` row with
216 `direction="increases"` AND `surface_accessibility != "no"`
217 (the textbook tumor-induced overexpression case)
218 
219 Picking `low` in these situations erases the targetable state the
220 catalog reader is filtering for. A protein with broad expression in
221 normal tissue PLUS cancer-induced surface upregulation is exactly
222 the case where the targetable signal is the *delta* — set
223 `state_dependence ∈ {moderate, high}` so the reader sees it.
224 
225 The orchestrator validates this rule post-hoc; a violation raises
226 rather than silently shipping a miscalibrated record.

244 AND `state_dependence ∈ {moderate, high}` — the targetable state is

254 `cytoplasmic`, `nuclear`, `mitochondrial_internal`,
255 `endomembrane_resident`, `nuclear_envelope`, `inner_leaflet_anchored`,
256 `secreted_only`, `pmhc_only_intracellular`, `other`. These are

258 accessible — useful catalog signal even on negative calls.
259 
260 **`endomembrane_resident` vs `dual_localization` — load-bearing
261 distinction.** Both apply to proteins whose canonical home is the
262 endomembrane system (TGN / ER / endosomes / lysosomes / etc.). The
263 test is whether the deep-dive evidence documents PM trafficking:
264 
265 * Literature shows transport carriers labelled with the protein
266 arriving at the PM, baseline PM-rim staining under normal
267 activity, or a measurable steady-state PM pool → pick
268 **`dual_localization`** (CONTEXTUAL bucket) +
269 `surface_accessibility=low` + `state_dependence ∈ {moderate, high}`.
270 The brief PM dwell is enough for an antibody to engage; the
271 targetable state IS the trafficking visit.
272 * Literature treats the protein as never reaching the PM (no
273 trafficking-to-PM evidence in the ledger, only intracellular
274 compartment residence) → pick **`endomembrane_resident`** (NO
275 bucket) + `surface_accessibility ∈ {no, low}`.
276 
277 Default to `dual_localization` whenever any trafficking-to-PM
278 observation exists in A1's ledger, even when most of the evidence
279 reads as intracellular — under-flagging the targetable PM-cycling
280 state is the worse failure mode for the catalog reader.

218## Non-canonical anchoring gate — non-TM proteins
219 
220When the input ledger or your trim notes indicate the protein has
221**no TM helix, no GPI anchor, no signal peptide for membrane insertion,
222no outer-leaflet anchor** — i.e. no canonical mechanism for sitting at
223the outer leaflet — you may still emit a `MethodObservation` with
224`accessibility_relevance=direct_surface_accessibility`, BUT only when
225the claim or quote explicitly identifies an **outer-leaflet** anchoring
226mechanism. Acceptable mechanisms (all place the protein on the
227extracellular face):
228 
229- partner-protein tethering (named TM partner whose extracellular
230 domain binds this protein at a named domain)
231- alternative GPI-anchored isoform (named isoform identifier)
232- β-barrel monotopic insertion at the outer leaflet
233- non-canonical surface translocation explicitly documented (the
234 paper names a specific trafficking pathway that delivers the
235 protein to the outer leaflet, demonstrated by extracellular
236 antibody binding or surface biotinylation)
237- palmitoylation at a named Cys **only when** the paper also names an
238 outer-leaflet retention signal (a TM partner, a signal peptide,
239 GPI). Palmitoylation alone is leaflet-agnostic — it can tether to
240 either face — so without an outer-leaflet qualifier it falls into
241 the inner-leaflet rejection below.
242 
243If no such mechanism is named, cap the row at
244`accessibility_relevance=supports_surface_localization` (cannot prove
245extracellular epitope reachable) and add a one-clause note in the
246observations field flagging "no anchoring mechanism named for non-TM
247protein". This forces the grader to confront *how* the protein is at the
248surface before granting a direct call — without locking out legitimate
249non-canonical anchored proteins where the mechanism is described.

251### Inner-leaflet evidence is NOT surface accessibility
252 
253A protein anchored to the **inner (cytoplasmic) leaflet** of the plasma
254membrane is at the PM but on the WRONG side — its body and epitopes
255hang into the cytoplasm and are not extracellularly accessible to a
256systemically delivered binder. Evidence that observes such a protein
257"at the plasma membrane" documents inner-leaflet association, not
258surface accessibility.
259 
260When the ledger names (or your trim notes flag) an inner-leaflet /
261cytoplasmic-facing anchor for this protein AND the assay observed it
262at the PM (live-cell imaging, FRAP, live-cell mutagenesis showing
263membrane-targeting loss in an anchoring-deficient mutant), cap the row
264at `accessibility_relevance=weak_or_ambiguous` and set
265`surface_claim_type=intracellular_pool`. Add a one-clause observation
266noting "inner-leaflet anchoring — not extracellularly accessible".
267Never promote such a row to `direct_surface_accessibility` or
268`supports_surface_localization`.
269 
270The non-permeabilized condition of an assay does NOT override this
271rule. Intact cells just mean the membrane is intact; the protein can
272still be on the cytoplasmic side of it.
273 
274Exception: when the SAME paper or a sibling claim documents a
275non-canonical OUTER-surface event for this protein via a named
276mechanism (per the "Non-canonical anchoring gate" above), emit a
277SEPARATE row at `direct_surface_accessibility` keyed to that outer-
278surface evidence. The inner-leaflet row stays `weak_or_ambiguous`.

338 
339`MethodObservation` has no `species` field of its own. Species lives

356 trafficking, so this field decides whether the evidence is real

400### Antibody-identifier extraction discipline
401 
402The input claim quote almost always names the antibody in some form;
403your job is to SPLIT the identifier into the right structured fields,
404not collapse it into `name`:
405 
406* **`clone`** — alphanumeric clone ID (examples: `528`, `4D6`,
407 `D38B1`, `43-14A`, `AB-101`, `H300`, `9G4`, `B-A18`). When
408 the quote says "clone 528", "528 antibody", "anti-TARGET clone 528",
409 "anti-TARGET (528)", set `clone="528"`.
410* **`vendor`** — company name when stated (examples: `BD Pharmingen`,
411 `BD Biosciences`, `Cell Signaling Technology`, `Abcam`,
412 `R&D Systems`, `Thermo Fisher`, `Santa Cruz`, `Sigma`, `BioLegend`,
413 `Invitrogen`, `Millipore`).
414* **`catalog`** — vendor catalog number when stated (examples:
415 `#9101S`, `ab32077`, `MAB1095`, `sc-9996`, `M0876`).
416* **`rrid`** — Research Resource Identifier when stated (examples:
417 `AB_2138158`, `RRID:AB_396171`). Strip the `RRID:` prefix when
418 present so the field is just the `AB_...` identifier.
419* **`name`** — short canonical label, NOT the clone or vendor. Use
420 `anti-TARGET` (the gene/protein the antibody recognizes). The name
421 field is for what the antibody recognizes, not for stuffing the
422 identifier in.

425`clone=null`, `vendor=null`
426**Good**: `name="anti-TARGET"`, `clone="528"`, `vendor="BD Biosciences"`,
