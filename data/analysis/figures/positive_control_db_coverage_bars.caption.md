**The triage agent recovers near-complete coverage of clinically-validated surface targets — including targets no individual database calls — across three orthogonal positive-control sets.** Each panel is one independently-curated set of cell-surface targets; each bar is the fraction that source labels surface: the five gating databases (UniProt and CSPA on their SurfaceBench-optimized cutoffs; GO CC, SURFY, HPA native) and the two-stage Sonnet 4.6 triage agent. (a) Antibody–drug-conjugate targets (n = 234): union of TheraSAbDab ADCs, Open Targets 26.06 ADC mechanism-of-action targets, and ADCdb antigens, HGNC-resolved, after removing non-cytotoxic conjugates, Open Targets family-expansion artifacts, and ADCdb secreted/intracellular antigens. (b) T-cell-engager targets (n = 30): TheraSAbDab CD3-bispecifics. (c) ViralZone human viral entry receptors (n = 62). Sonnet covers 233/234 (99.6%), 30/30 (100%), and 61/62 (98.4%) respectively — exceeding the best single database (UniProt/SURFY, 85–93%) in every panel. Eight ADC targets and one TCE target lie in zero of the five databases yet are recovered by Sonnet (ASPH, FMOD, GSDME, HSPA1A, KLK2, KLK3, LRG1, MMP9) — contextual-surface biology the databases' steady-state definitions miss. The two residual misses are coherent: CT83 (a cancer/testis antigen) and NPC1 (the Ebola entry receptor, dominantly late-endosomal).
