**Re-deriving each source database's surface-inclusion rule against SurfaceBench changes which proteins two of the five sources admit, while three are already well-calibrated.** Each panel shows one source database; points are alternative inclusion rules of increasing permissiveness, plotted by the number of proteins each rule would admit (horizontal axis, log scale; further left is stricter) against classification accuracy on SurfaceBench, the 147-protein benchmark of manually labeled surface, contextually surface, and non-surface proteins (vertical axis). Each point is also annotated with the fraction of true surface proteins recovered and the fraction of non-surface proteins correctly excluded, making the recall-versus-specificity trade-off behind each accuracy value explicit. The current rule and the rule recommended after the benchmark audit are marked. Two sources were recalibrated. UniProt was loosened from 3,175 to 3,894 admitted proteins by additionally accepting transmembrane- and signal-peptide-annotated entries, recovering 719 GPI-anchored and single-pass receptors it had been missing and raising accuracy from 78% to 89% with little loss of specificity. CSPA was tightened from 1,286 to 1,039 by restricting it to high-confidence detections only, improving specificity at comparable recall. The GO cellular-component, Human Protein Atlas, and SURFY rules were already at their accuracy optimum and were left unchanged.
