Executive summary

CD81 is a tetraspanin (4TM) with two extracellular loops (SEL, LEL) that is robustly surface-accessible across most human cell types. Evidence spans live-cell flow cytometry on primary AML blasts and multiple cell lines (with CRISPR-KO controls), membrane-impermeable surface biotinylation MS on glioblastoma lines and PBMCs, immunogold EM on EVs, and a foundational proteolysis topology assay. Surface expression is broadly constitutive but shows meaningful disease-state modulation: elevated on non-CBF AML blasts (prognostic and LSC-enriched), upregulated under GBM hypoxia, and absent on wild-type HepG2 cells. The tetraspanin web engagement (CD19 co-receptor dependency) and antibody-driven internalization represent the primary accessibility risks.

(6) Antibody-mediated surface down-modulation on live hematolymphoid cells (PMID:2398277). No meaningful contradictions identified; cytosol annotation in HPA is consistent with known tetraspanin intracellular pool and does not conflict with surface presence.
