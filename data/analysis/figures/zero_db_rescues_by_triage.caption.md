**Figure 3. The triage agent rescues 960 surface candidates that no source database flags.** The zero-database slice holds genes that none of the five reference sources (UniProt, GO cellular component, the Human Protein Atlas, SURFY, and CSPA) marks as surface-localized under the bench-optimized cutoffs, the same thresholds the accuracy figures use. Within it the two-stage Sonnet 4.6 triage agent calls proteins definitively surface ("yes", panel a) or conditionally surface ("contextual": state- or lineage-dependent, panel b). The agent first reviews NCBI gene and protein records, then re-examines genes rejected for an ambiguous reason with a literature-search pass. Bars group each verdict by the agent's rationale, with representative examples labeled. Every one is documented in the primary literature as reaching the cell surface (flow cytometry, surface biotinylation, or equivalent), yet flagged by no database even under the optimized cutoffs.
