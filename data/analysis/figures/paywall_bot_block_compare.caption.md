**Casting a wider literature net surfaces several times more papers per gene, but most of the additional papers are unreachable, which is why the broader strategy was not adopted.** Two discovery strategies were compared on the same randomly sampled genes through the same retrieval chain: the production strategy, which searches EuropePMC and adds machine-annotated PubTator mentions, NCBI's curated gene-to-publication links, and standing topic queries; and a broad alternative issuing one gene-name query plus method-keyword queries against the OpenAlex index, which also reaches preprints and non-PubMed journals. OpenAlex discovered far more papers per gene, but reachability collapsed on the larger pool, adding mostly inaccessible literature at proportionally higher cost, so production discovery was kept.
