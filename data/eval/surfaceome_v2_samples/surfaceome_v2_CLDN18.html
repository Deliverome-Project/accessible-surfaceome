Executive summary

CLDN18 (principally isoform CLDN18.2) is a multi-pass transmembrane tight-junction protein with high, direct-multi-method evidence for surface accessibility on tumor cells. Live-cell flow cytometry on intact cancer cells, 89Zr-zolbetuximab PET in xenografts, and membranous IHC across multiple tumor cohorts converge on confirmed ECD exposure. Surface accessibility is strongly state-dependent: in normal differentiated gastric mucosa the ECD is junctionally sequestered; malignant transformation disrupts tight junctions and exposes the ECD. An FDA-approved antibody (zolbetuximab) directly validates the therapeutically accessible surface form. Key risks are junctional subdomain sequestration in normal tissue and uncertain localization of the CLDN18-ARHGAP26 fusion protein in a minority of tumors.

grade rationale
CLDN18 surface accessibility is supported by multiple independent direct methods from multiple sources. (1) Live-cell flow cytometry on intact N87 cells ectopically expressing CLDN18.1 and CLDN18.2 (PMC11712040); (2) Live-cell FACS binding assays with anti-CLDN18.2 antibodies on intact tumor cells, explicitly no permeabilization (PMC12323529, PMC12808354, PMID19047087); (3) In vivo functional imaging: 89Zr-zolbetuximab PET in xenograft models showing specific tumor uptake significantly above IgG control, directly confirming ECD accessibility in vivo (PMC12766881); (4) Membranous IHC (non-permeabilized, membranous-only scoring) across multiple tumor cohorts (PMC12018027, PMC11979426, PMC12078809, PMC12975664). Two minor ambiguities exist: IHC scoring guidelines for 1+ vs 2+ are noted as imprecise, and the CLDN18-ARHGAP26 fusion protein's surface localization is uncertain. These are minor caveats that do not undermine the robust multi-method direct evidence for wildtype CLDN18.2 surface accessibility.

Therapeutic engagement

stage: approved_drug
description
Zolbetuximab (anti-CLDN18.2 monoclonal antibody, FDA-approved for gastric/GEJ adenocarcinoma) is the lead therapeutic engaging CLDN18.2 at the cell surface. Clinical eligibility requires ≥75% of tumor cells with moderate-to-strong membranous IHC staining (SPOTLIGHT/GLOW trial criteria). Additional programs include CAR-T therapies targeting surface CLDN18.2, and preclinical radiolabeled formats (89Zr/177Lu-zolbetuximab) for PET imaging and radioligand therapy in gastric and PDAC xenograft models. Multiple isoform-selective anti-CLDN18.2 antibodies (e.g., hVH6) have been developed with counter-screening against CLDN18.1.
surface-form rationale
CLDN18 is a multi-pass transmembrane tight-junction protein with no documented secreted or shed isoform in this ledger. All therapeutics engage the membrane-anchored form. Importantly, in normal differentiated gastric epithelium CLDN18.2's extracellular domain is buried within tight junctions and inaccessible; tumor disruption of tight junctions exposes the ECD at the free cell surface, enabling antibody and CAR-T engagement. Only the membrane-anchored surface form is relevant for therapeutic targeting.
