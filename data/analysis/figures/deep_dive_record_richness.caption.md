**Record richness scales with deep-dive confidence tier.** This figure summarises a gene's deep-dive record along five richness axes, each shown as the real per-gene distribution faceted by deep-dive tier (canonical, likely, low, no): (a) papers found in discovery, (b) papers selected for full-text reading, (c) selected papers with extracellular evidence, (d) evidence records extracted, and (e) deterministic features populated (0–6). Every violin is the actual distribution of that tier's genes — a median line runs inside each violin and faint dots show the real per-tier values, so higher-confidence tiers visibly draw on a deeper evidence base. Panels (a) and (b) keep the non-surface "no" tier; panels (c)–(e) drop it because non-surface proteins carry little extracellular evidence by definition. The "uncertain" tier (n=9) is dropped everywhere as too small to plot a distribution. PRELIMINARY: 1,175 of the roughly 5,128 candidate genes have been swept so far and this is pre-QA-fix; the figure re-renders from the real per-gene counts as the sweep completes.
