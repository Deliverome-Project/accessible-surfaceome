**Figure 1. Five gold-standard surface-protein resources agree on only 188 proteins genome-wide.** Five-way ellipse Venn of per-source surface-protein membership across the 19,324 protein-coding genes in the candidate universe. Each set is the proteins one source marks as cell-surface under its native, pre-recalibration inclusion rule: UniProt (n = 3,092), strict surface terms or extracellular topology; SURFY (n = 2,733), the published binary classifier call; HPA (n = 2,050), Enhanced/Supported/Approved reliability for plasma membrane or cell junctions; GO CC (n = 1,998), experimentally supported (non-IEA) annotation to a surface-relevant cellular-component term; and CSPA (n = 1,231), high-confidence or putative cell-surface calls. The all-five intersection holds 188 proteins, under 10% of the average source's membership, while the union of all five reaches 5,546. Each source also contributes a large private fraction unique to it, led by HPA (1,063 proteins in no other source) and SURFY (917 shared only with UniProt), reflecting their distinct evidence types — antibody-based subcellular imaging and machine-learning prediction. Counts use each source's native definitions; the SurfaceBench-tuned recalibration applied downstream (UniProt broadened to admit transmembrane and signal-peptide proteins, CSPA restricted to high-confidence calls) is not shown here. Region labels below 100 proteins are suppressed for legibility; per-source totals in the legend are exact.
