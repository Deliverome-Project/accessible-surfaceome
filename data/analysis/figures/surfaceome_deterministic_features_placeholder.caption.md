**Across the deep-dived surfaceome, the nine precomputed deterministic features distribute as expected for bona-fide surface proteins, and the pattern already tracks the real deep-dive confidence tier.** Each deep-dived gene is faceted by its real deep-dive surface-accessibility tier, collapsed into three comparison groups: canonical (the high-confidence surface tier), likely, and below-likely (the low, uncertain, and no tiers pooled). Genes that are Sonnet-positive but not yet deep-dived carry a pending tier and are excluded from this per-tier comparison, since they have no deep-dive verdict to compare. The 3×3 grid compares one deterministic feature per panel: DeepTMHMM transmembrane-helix count and protein length (boxplots), and the fraction carrying a signal peptide, an extracellular N-terminus, an extracellular C-terminus, a high-confidence 1:1 mouse ortholog, a high-confidence 1:1 cyno ortholog (Ensembl Compara), a predicted homo-oligomer state (Schweke 2024), and an alternative isoform with a topology differing from canonical. Each is a tool-derived value on the immutable deterministic_features record block, reproducible from the bundled per-gene TSV. PRELIMINARY: about 1,197 of the roughly 5,128 candidate genes have been deep-dived so far, and the below-likely tier is weak-evidence-inflated by the pretrim bug, so the per-tier rates should be read as provisional until the sweep completes and the QA fix lands.
