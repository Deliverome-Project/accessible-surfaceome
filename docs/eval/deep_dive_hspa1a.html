Rationale

Sonnet's synthesis across the evidence pool.

HSPA1A (HSP70/HSP72, 641 aa) is canonically a cytoplasmic-nuclear molecular chaperone with no TM domain, no signal peptide, and no GPI anchor. All 8 M1 source databases vote false, consistent with its canonical intracellular role. However, a large body of primary evidence — surface biotinylation of intact viable tumor cells (~15-20% of total cellular Hsp70 detected on the plasma membrane), flow cytometry with the tumor-selective cmHsp70.1 mAb on non-permeabilized cells across colorectal, lung, pancreas, and neuronal carcinomas and leukemia, immunofluorescence in heat-shocked cancer cells, and flow cytometry during immunogenic cell death — establishes conditional outer-leaflet surface localization in transformed and stressed cells. Normal cells (except spermatogenic) are strictly surface-negative. Membrane anchorage is lipid-mediated: Gb3 glycosphingolipid in cholesterol-rich microdomains and heat-shock-induced phosphatidylserine accumulation drive peripheral association with the outer leaflet without a classical TM helix. When surface-localized, the full 641 aa protein — including the C-terminal substrate-binding domain confirmed accessible by cmHsp70.1 mAb epitope mapping — is extracellularly exposed. UniProt additionally annotates HSPA1A as Secreted; circulating eHSP70 is documented, representing a soluble decoy pool. Preclinical extracellular-engagement validation includes cmHsp70.1 antibody binding on live tumor cells and the TKD peptide-NK cell therapeutic approach targeting membrane HSP70. Classification: conditional_surface, outer_leaflet_peripheral, peripheral anchor, exposed_ecd.

Confidence reasoning

All 8 M1 database sources vote false — HSPA1A lacks TM domain, signal peptide, and GPI anchor, so canonical surface-prediction pipelines correctly reject it. However, primary experimental literature (surface biotinylation on intact viable tumor cells, flow cytometry with cmHsp70.1 mAb on non-permeabilized cells, and immunofluorescence confocal microscopy) robustly establishes conditional outer-leaflet localization in transformed cells and under heat/ICD stress. Normal cells are consistently surface-negative. The mechanism (peripheral lipid anchoring via Gb3 glycosphingolipid and phosphatidylserine in cholesterol-rich microdomains, non-classical secretory pathway) is well characterized. The conditional nature (strictly absent from normal cells), non-canonical membrane anchor, and substantial circulating soluble pool each reduce confidence relative to a constitutively surface-expressed protein with a canonical TM helix. Medium confidence is appropriate: surface evidence is strong for the oncogenic/stress contexts but not for a baseline surface call.

Risk flags

Structured risks (shedding / secreted form) flagged for downstream consumers.

secreted_form medium

HSPA1A is actively released into the extracellular space via a non-classical lysosomal-endosomal pathway (UniProt: Secreted). Elevated circulating eHSP70 is documented in cancer, sepsis, and exercise/heat-stress states. This soluble pool may compete with extracellular binders targeting membrane-anchored mHSP70, reducing effective surface engagement.

cited evidence: evi_001
