**SurfaceBench over-samples the membrane topologies where databases disagree, so its measured accuracy is a conservative floor on full-catalog performance.** Each gene is scored across nine membrane-topology classes — GPI-anchored, 7-transmembrane GPCR, multi-pass transmembrane, single-pass transmembrane, likely secreted, inner-leaflet lipidated, no transmembrane or signal, glycosylation-site-bearing, and transmembrane-without-signal-peptide — and the per-class composition of the benchmark (99 hand-curated genes — the benchmark's ground-truth yes/contextual surface-positive calls, all carrying topology annotations) is plotted against the shipped accessible set (4,426 genes, the two-tier yes/contextual universe the production pipeline ships after its literature sweep plus rescue pass). Bars give the percentage of each cohort in each class; error bars on the benchmark are Wilson 95% binomial confidence intervals, while the full set is treated as a population and carries none. Each class is tested with an exact two-tailed binomial test of the benchmark count against the population proportion, Bonferroni-corrected across the nine classes. Three classes reach significance: the benchmark is enriched for glycosylation-site-bearing proteins (+20.5 pp, ***) and GPI-anchored proteins (+14.1 pp, ***) and depleted for multi-pass transmembrane proteins (−11.2 pp, *). Because the benchmark is deliberately weighted toward the ambiguous, database-disagreement-prone topologies and away from the easy multi-pass case, accuracy measured on it is a lower bound on accuracy across the full accessible set.
