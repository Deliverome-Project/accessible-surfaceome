**Figure 3. The triage agent rescues 1,042 surface candidates that no source database flags.** Of the 19,324 protein-coding genes in the whole-proteome catalog, 13,778 (71%) sit in the zero-database slice where none of the five reference sources (UniProt, GO cellular component, the Human Protein Atlas, SURFY, and CSPA) marks the protein as surface-localized. Within that slice the two-stage Sonnet 4.6 triage agent calls 121 proteins definitively surface ("yes", panel a) and 921 conditionally surface ("contextual": state- or lineage-dependent, panel b). The agent first reviews each gene using NCBI gene and protein records; genes it initially rejects for an ambiguous reason are re-examined with a literature-search pass, which upgraded 177 of the 1,042 rescues (the remaining 865 came from the first pass). Bars group each verdict by the agent's stated rationale — classical receptors, multipass channels with exposed loops, GPI-anchored and stable-complex partners on the definite side; dual-localized, tissue-restricted, surface-tethered, cell-state-induced, and secretion-exposed proteins on the contextual side. Listed examples (PVRIG, STEAP1, CRIPTO, ORAI2, LY96; IL15, TIMP2, GSDMD, HSPA1A, HSP90B1, HPSE) each belong to the reason category shown above them.
