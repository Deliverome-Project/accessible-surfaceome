**Figure 1. Five gold-standard surface-protein databases agree on only 188 proteins genome-wide.** Five-way ellipse Venn over the initial (pre-recalibration) binary surface flag from each of the five sources used in this study — UniProt (n = 3,092), SURFY (n = 2,733), HPA (n = 2,050), GO CC (n = 1,998), and CSPA (n = 1,231) — built from the whole-proteome catalog (`data/processed/catalog/whole_proteome_catalog.tsv`, 19,324 protein-coding genes from the candidate universe). Each set is the union of all UniProt accessions a source labels as plasma-membrane surface under the source-specific initial rule documented in Methods (UniProt: strict surface terms or extracellular topology; GO CC: non-IEA annotation to a surface-relevant CC term; HPA: Enhanced/Supported/Approved reliability for Plasma membrane or Cell Junctions; SURFY: published binary classifier output; CSPA: high-confidence or putative). The SurfaceBench-tuned recalibration that the Methods applies downstream (UniProt expanded to admit transmembrane/signal-peptide proteins, CSPA tightened to high-confidence only) is not shown here — this panel deliberately uses the pre-recalibration flags so the overlap reflects each source's native definition. The central all-five intersection contains 188 proteins (< 10% of the average source's catalog), while every source contributes its own large source-private slice — most strikingly HPA (1,063 not in any other source) and SURFY (917 only shared with UniProt), reflecting their distinct evidence modalities (antibody-validated subcellular imaging and ML prediction, respectively). Region labels with fewer than 100 proteins are suppressed for readability; per-source totals in the legend are exact. The poor overlap motivates the LLM-based triage approach: no single source captures the consensus surfaceome, and unioning all five inflates membership at the cost of definitional coherence.
